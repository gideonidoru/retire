The FERS/BRS formula hasn't changed since TSP began in 1987: automatic 1% + full match on your first 3% + half on the next 2% — agency max 5%, yours at 5%. What varies by hire date is your FERS pension withholding (0.8% / 3.1% / 4.4%), which doesn't touch the match. Roth and traditional receive the same match (always traditional). Outside traditional IRAs or old 401(k)s? Add them to this balance — and Roth IRAs into the Roth share — they follow the same tax and RMD treatment. Growth above inflation keeps everything in today's dollars; the 4% withdrawal rule is a 30-year starting point — early retirees often use 3–3.5%.

How it computes

The frame

Everything is in today's dollars: growth and draw rates are "above inflation," so a dollar anywhere on the chart buys what a dollar buys now. One state object feeds one pure computation; every number on the page derives from it, every time anything changes.

The streams

FERS: high-3 × creditable years × multiplier (1.0%; 1.1% at 62+ with 20 years, sick leave counting per OPM BAL 18-103; 1.7% special provisions), with MRA and every eligibility path derived from your birthdate and SCD — including MRA+10 reductions, VERA early-outs (immediate unreduced, supplement from the MRA, VSIP into savings), deferred commencement by service (30 years → MRA unreduced, 20 → 60, else 62), disability phases, and survivor election costs. The supplement uses civilian years only. No FERS COLA before 62 and the diet COLA after are modeled as real erosion at the inflation dial. Military: your DFAS gross (or high-3/BRS formula, 75% cap), SBP premium deducted if elected, and the sub-50% VA waiver unless CRSC. VA: official-pattern rate tables with the full dependent matrix — or your award letter, verbatim. Social Security: your statement's FRA figure, trimmed if you stop earning early (bend-point inversion), then SSA's exact monthly claim factors. TSP & savings: a year-by-year simulation — contributions (with IRS catch-up step-ups), growth, your draw schedule, one-time withdrawals, and RMDs forced from 73 on the traditional share.

Taxes & premiums

Federal brackets with the standard deduction (65+ add included), up-to-85% Social Security taxability via the provisional-income formula, your state's actual retirement-income treatment (or host-country treatment abroad), Medicare Part B with IRMAA tiers from 65, health premiums, and the 10% early-withdrawal penalty where the Rule of 55 doesn't reach. The dotted line on the income chart is the year-by-year result.

The recommendation

Set a goal and the engine walks every retirement age 40–70, requiring the goal covered in every single year to your horizon — gap years included. The horizon comes from an SSA life table (age, sex, income-adjusted; the longer-lived spouse in a household) plus a buffer. All of it is adjustable in the dials.

Verification

The math is locked by automated tests, including goldens against published OPM computation examples, SSA's exact reduction factors, the TSP match schedule, and the DFAS formula. Source is public — github.com/gideonidoru/retire. Every simplification is labeled where it applies, with the direction of its error where known.
Disclosures & privacy

What this tool is

Assembled produces estimates and observations from the numbers you enter — it is not financial, legal, tax, or investment advice, and it is not a recommendation to retire, claim, buy back service, or elect anything. Decisions of this size deserve verification against your official sources: OPM, DFAS, VA, SSA, and TSP statements, and where it matters, a professional who can see your whole picture.

Known simplifications

Every figure is in today's dollars; growth assumptions are "above inflation." Your current salary stands in for the FERS high-3 (a conservative floor). VA rates use approximate 2026 tables. Social Security uses an approximate claim-age curve and, for pasted earnings tables, approximate wage indexing. The buyback deposit cost, CRDP/CRSC detail, taxes, and the FERS COLA gap before 62 are not yet modeled — each is flagged in place where it applies. Lifespan is a statistical estimate from public life tables adjusted for income; it is a planning horizon, not a prediction about you.

Privacy

Your data never leaves your device. There is no server, no account, no analytics, and no tracking. Everything you type is computed in your browser and saved only to this browser's local storage; "Start over" erases it. Exports (JSON, CSV, PDF) are generated locally and go only where you put them. The single network request this page makes is for its typefaces from Google Fonts, which contains nothing you've entered. The downloadable single-file version makes no requests at all once saved.
